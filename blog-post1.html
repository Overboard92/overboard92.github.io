Apple's Spaceship Campus

Welcome aboard  the mothership!!

FACTS:

- 175-acre campus

- Over 2.8 million sq. ft. of office space

- 17 megawatts of rooftop solar

- Separate on-site R&D facilities

- 100,000 sq. ft. fitness center

- Underground 1000-seat Steve Jobs Theater

- Public Visitor Center featuring Augmented Reality experience

- Will run on 100% renewable energy

- Architected by Foster+Partners

The new campus is officially named Apple Park, located in Cupertino, California.

The building is designed to house some 13,000 employees. The land cost was estimated at $160 million. In 2011, the budget was less than $3 billion. However, in 2013 the total cost was estimated to be closer to $5 billion.

Steve Jobs wanted the whole campus to look less like an office park and more like a nature refuge. Eighty percent of the site consists of green space planted with drought-resistant trees and plants indigenous to the Cupertino area, and the center courtyard of the main building features an artificial pond.

The headquarters gained unfavorable attention when it emerged recently (2018) that two workers had been injured and required hospital treatment after walking into the building's clear glass walls and doors.
